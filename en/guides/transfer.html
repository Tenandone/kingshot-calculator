Kingshot Kingdom Transfer Guide

This detailed guide covers the basic kingdom transfer conditions, the difference between normal and special invites, transfer power limits, practical admin-side operating priorities, and the key checkpoints players should review before moving.

This page is more than a simple rule summary. It is a practical guide based on real server management and transfer preparation, focused on who to accept, where to move, and what to confirm first.

Overview

Kingshot kingdom transfer is a system that lets you move your city to another kingdom within the same progression group. On the surface, it may look like you only need transfer tickets and basic requirements, but in practice, server atmosphere, admin style, special invite availability, transfer power limits, and alliance placement all matter.

During a Kingshot transfer season, the most desired players are not always just the strongest ones. Adaptability, communication, alliance fit, possible conflict with existing members, and admin trust can all affect the outcome. That is why a strong transfer guide should explain not only the rules, but also both the admin perspective and the migrant perspective.

Key point: Transfer is not just about moving. It is also a process where a server and alliance choose people. Along with meeting the rules, reputation, communication, adaptability, and trust with admins matter a lot.

Core Rules

Basic transfer structure

  • Transfer is only possible within the same progression group.
  • Kingdoms may be divided into stronger kingdoms and regular kingdoms, with different intake structures.
  • Normal invites and special invites serve different roles and are separated by the transfer power limit.

Conditions before transfer

  • If you exceed the transfer power limit, moving is generally difficult without special handling.
  • You must meet basic conditions such as City Center level, non-combat status, alliance withdrawal, and transfer cooldown.
  • You should also check character count in the target kingdom and the number of transfer tickets you have.

Normal Invite vs Special Invite

Understanding the difference between a Kingshot special invite and a normal invite makes transfer-season decisions much easier. In particular, if a player exceeds the Kingshot transfer power limit, a special invite is required. That means admins need to plan carefully who should receive one, and players should understand which category they fall into first.

Practical point

A special invite is not a card you use automatically just because someone has high power. Server atmosphere and alliance fit matter too, and if used badly, it can even push existing members away.

If You Are an Admin

1. Even if you are not a whale, you need to build your position first if you want to influence transfer direction

You need to build your standing both inside your alliance and across the server, while also showing major server players how valuable you are. The key point here is simple: people matter. Being respectful to all players and avoiding unnecessary server-wide conflict is important.

Powerful players are still people, and most of them prefer to play with someone who is easy to work with. That is why your daily behavior often determines how much influence you will really have during transfer season. This is one reason why spending alone does not decide everything.

2. You need to set transfer priorities

When transfer season begins, you may get far more applications than expected. That is why it is efficient to organize things in advance in a memo or spreadsheet, recording player ID, server, desired alliance, whether they have a recommender, and similar details. In practice, players who join your server or alliance chat early and adapt well often deserve higher priority.

Example priority order
  • Priority 1: Strong whales with good character
  • Priority 2: Players who communicate well with alliance members
  • Priority 3: People wanted by whales or their close groups already coming over
  • Priority 4: Everyone else

In the end, you need ongoing communication with the king and server leadership to confirm how many special invites can be used and how many normal invites are available for this season.

3. If you want to bring in whales as a group move, cross-check anyone they recommend

There are more cases than people think where one problematic person made alliance members leave. Around transfer time, servers often become full of rumors and drama. So even if a whale wants someone, it is still important to check with other whales and existing server members before accepting them.

If someone would place too much burden on the alliance as a whole, moving them lower on the priority list is also a valid management choice. Transfer is not only about accepting one player. It is about whether the existing group can keep moving forward together after that player arrives.

4. If you want to recruit whales, present a project

The bigger the spender, the longer they tend to hesitate if the transfer does not look efficient. That is why admins should explain the alliance direction, management style, NAP structure, event standards, and overall server atmosphere like a clear project.

Saying just come here is not enough. You need to show what kind of war environment they will join, what role they will play, and what kind of server you are trying to build. That creates trust, because if they come over, they will strengthen not only your alliance but the whole server.

If You Are Preparing to Transfer

1. Check whether the target server is stable

Always cross-check. This is likely to be a place where you stay for at least a month or longer, so before looking at raw power, check the server atmosphere, war frequency, the number of aggressive players, and how stable the NAP environment really is.

2. Look at the admin style

Check whether the admins genuinely care about their alliance members and whether their words match their actions. It matters whether they are only kind during transfer season or whether their management is consistently solid year-round.

3. The alliance you want to join must fit you

Some alliances are strict with rules, while others are much more relaxed under NAP structures. If the alliance culture does not match your personality, it becomes hard to stay long-term no matter how strong you are.

4. Check the alliance position inside the server

If the alliance has a strong position, you are less likely to suffer from sudden attacks or server-wide conflicts. Do not look only at rankings. Also check how protected and respected that alliance actually is within the kingdom.

Extra point: Normal invites and special invites are not just numbers. They are also a form of promise. How a server uses them often reflects its trustworthiness and operating style.

If you are preparing for a Kingshot kingdom transfer, do not look only at power. Check whether the server wants you enough to use a Kingshot special invite, what kind of adjustments may be needed to fit the Kingshot transfer power limit, and whether the target alliance actually matches you. In the end, long-lasting transfers are decided by environment, not just numbers.

FAQ

Is transfer only possible within the same group?

Yes. In general, kingdom transfer is only possible within the same progression group. That is why the first thing you should check is whether the target kingdom is in the same group as your server.

Who should receive a special invite?

Usually, special invites are prioritized for key players who exceed the power limit, players with strong server value, or people who are essential to an alliance recruitment project.

Does higher power automatically increase priority?

Not always. Personality, communication, fit with existing members, server adaptability, and even the trust level of the recommender can have a major impact on real priority.

What matters most when choosing a server to transfer to?

Stability. More than raw ranking, you should look at the server atmosphere, admin management style, alliance position, attack frequency, and how stable the NAP environment really is.

What You Should Know Before a Kingshot Kingdom Transfer

Kingshot kingdom transfer is not content you can solve simply by preparing transfer tickets. In practice, you also need to match server atmosphere, special invite usage, communication with admins, alliance position, and transfer power limit adjustments if you want the outcome you actually want.

That is why players looking for a Kingshot transfer guide should pay attention not only to the rules, but also to which servers are stable, which alliances fit their own style, and whether the admin team is trustworthy. In particular, Kingshot special invites and Kingshot normal invites can also show how strongly a server actually wants certain players, which means that during transfer season, relationships and trust can matter more than raw numbers.
