Kingshot Eternity’s Reach Guide · Vein Capture, Palace Timing, and Skill Rotation

Eternity’s Reach is a solo event that usually opens every two weeks. The entire mode is about collecting as many Bronze Stones as possible. If you understand vein capture, Cesare guards, Unstable Vein timings, Palace decisions, and the skill route, you can place much more consistently.

Core takeaway

  • Stronger accounts focus on level 3 veins and guards, while weaker accounts should rely on stable level 1 to 2 gathering loops.
  • The recommended skill route is Right-Right-Left-Left-Right, with key timings at 20/15/10/5 minutes.
  • Unstable Veins and hand-drop pickup speed have a huge impact on final rankings.

Remember these first

  • Your real goal is maximizing Bronze Stone income. Pathing and timing matter more than fighting itself.
  • Stronger players should focus on level 3 veins, Cesare guards, and Palace entry.
  • Weaker players should repeat safer level 1 to 2 vein captures and only take favorable guard fights.
  • Unstable Veins should be contested immediately when they appear.
  • The 15-minute skill timing alone can create a huge score gap.

Most common mistakes

  • Wasting the opening 3 minutes and ending up stuck rotating weak veins.
  • Sending weak heroes into guards and getting locked out for 5 minutes.
  • Missing the Unstable Vein alert and losing the most valuable Bronze Stone window.
  • Forcing Palace fights on an account that cannot hold them, then breaking your entire gathering loop.

1) Basics and objective

Eternity’s Reach is a solo event that usually appears on a two-week cycle and lasts for 30 minutes. The main objective is to collect as many Bronze Stones as possible for Lord gem progression.

  • Veins come in levels 1 through 3.
  • Higher-level veins give more Bronze Stones, but they are also harder to hold.
  • Capturing a vein creates a hand-drop Bronze Stone pickup that anyone can collect immediately.
  • That means the mode is not just about gathering. It is about capture + pickup recovery together.

The simplest way to understand it

Holding a vein for a long time matters less than quickly repeating strong captures and collecting the drops efficiently.

2) Core strategy by account strength

For stronger accounts

  • Secure level 3 vein clusters early
  • Farm Cesare guards repeatedly
  • Aim for skill level 5 before the 20-minute mark
  • Prepare for Palace entry around 7 minutes remaining

For F2P / lower-power accounts

  • Use stable level 1 to 2 vein loops
  • Only take safe guard fights
  • Usually skip Palace contests
  • Prioritize Unstable Veins and drop pickups

Use the opening 3-minute wait properly

The first 3 minutes should not be wasted. Use that time to check where the level 2 to 3 veins and guard clusters are. Then relocate your city into a better area so your early rotation starts strong.

Why Cesare guards matter

  • They give skill EXP when killed.
  • If you lose, that hero becomes unusable for 5 minutes.
  • That is why stronger accounts should actively farm them, while weaker accounts should be selective.

3) Palace decisions

The Palace can be a major score source for stronger players, but for weaker accounts it can completely destroy an otherwise stable run. That is why you should decide beforehand whether your account should even contest it.

  • Stronger players: usually begin preparing for the central Palace at around 7 minutes remaining.
  • 50% march speedups used 3 to 4 times can often let you arrive with roughly 2 seconds left, depending on map distance.
  • Weaker players: are usually better off skipping Palace fights and staying focused on vein gathering and pickups.

Palace is optional, not mandatory

If your account cannot realistically hold Palace, forcing it usually ruins your vein loop and your guard rhythm at the same time.

4) Skill tree and timing

Recommended skill route

Right - Right - Left - Left - Right

  1. Lv1 (Right): extra Bronze Stones when killing guards
  2. Lv2 (Right): troop movement speed +25%
  3. Lv3 (Left): extra Bronze Stones on successful capture (1-minute cooldown)
  4. Lv4 (Left): gathering efficiency +15%
  5. Lv5 (Right): gathering speed +50% for 60 seconds (5-minute cooldown)

Most important timings

  • 20 minutes remaining
  • 15 minutes remaining
  • 10 minutes remaining
  • 5 minutes remaining

Practical note

  • Just getting the 15-minute timing right can make a very noticeable difference.
  • Your target is to reach skill level 5 before the 20-minute point.
  • That means your early guard farming needs to be efficient.

5) Unstable Vein timing

Unstable Veins gather faster, which makes them the highest-priority veins in the mode.

  • They usually appear around 20, 19, and 18 minutes remaining, then again around 10, 9, and 8 minutes remaining.
  • As soon as the alert appears, send all available marches as quickly as possible.
  • After taking the vein, recover the hand-drop immediately so you get the full value.

Standard Unstable Vein flow

See spawn → send all marches immediately → capture → collect hand-drops → rotate to the next target

6) Practical tips and routing

Build a circular route

Do not think of veins, guards, and hand-drops as separate tasks. Treat them as one circular loop. A clean rotation usually performs better than sitting in one zone for too long.

Weak heroes should gather, not gamble

If a hero has a real chance of losing to a guard, do not risk it. Let that hero focus on gathering and hand-drop recovery instead. A 5-minute lockout can break your whole run.

Leave overheated zones quickly

Fighting over one crowded area is not always efficient. In many cases, rotating to a nearby alternative vein is stronger. For F2P accounts especially, claiming open space is usually better than forcing direct fights.

Important warning

If you lose to a Cesare guard, that hero is locked for 5 minutes. Do not throw your main lineup into low-confidence fights.

7) Final summary

  • Stronger accounts: level 3 veins + guards + Palace entry at around 7 minutes remaining
  • Weaker accounts: stable level 1 to 2 vein loop + safe guard kills
  • Skill order: Right - Right - Left - Left - Right
  • Skill timings: 20 / 15 / 10 / 5 minutes
  • Main score swing: Unstable Vein alerts and hand-drop pickup speed

The real one-line conclusion

If you firmly remember Right-Right-Left-Left-Right and 20/15/10/5 minutes, your Eternity’s Reach performance improves immediately.

FAQ

How should F2P players approach Eternity’s Reach?

F2P or lower-power players usually do better by focusing on level 1 to 2 vein loops and safe guard kills instead of forcing Palace fights.

What is the best skill order in Eternity’s Reach?

The standard recommended route is Right-Right-Left-Left-Right, focusing first on guard kill rewards, then movement speed, capture bonus, gathering efficiency, and finally gathering speed.

When do Unstable Veins appear?

Unstable Veins usually appear around 20, 19, and 18 minutes remaining, then again around 10, 9, and 8 minutes remaining. When the alert appears, send your available marches immediately.
